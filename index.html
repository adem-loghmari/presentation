Act I

The Problem

Why generic e-commerce breaks the moment a venue has seats.

Context

Events are spatial and real-time

Off-the-shelf platforms are neither. Four pains follow:

  • Fragmented tools — separate apps for design, sales, door scanning.
  • No spatial rendering — they can't draw a custom seating chart.
  • Double-booking — a wide seat-lock window is a catastrophic race.
  • Walled gardens — high commissions, no embeddable API.
The central question

How do you let an organizer draw a venue once, and let the world book it in real time — without ever double-booking a seat?

Organizer · Create the event

It starts with an event

  • The root entity — title, timezone-aware UTC dates, an owner.
  • Holds encrypted payment credentials & the staff roster.
Organizer · Build the venue

Draw it seat by seat

  • Konva.js 2D canvas at 60 FPS — viewport culling + memoization.
  • Magic-Wand labels 500 seats in < 1 s; AI text-to-map from a prompt.
Organizer · Schedule sessions

Sessions on the calendar

  • A venue hosts multiple TimeSlots (e.g. 2pm & 8pm).
  • Each owns its own Locations — availability isolated per session, with a sales window.
Organizer · Publish

Publish — the canvas becomes data

  • Going public runs syncLocations(): canvas JSON → Locations + PriceTiers.
  • Three passes, keyed on stable IDs — orphans pruned automatically.
Organizer · Payment setup

Plug in payments

  • SkipCash keys stored encrypted at rest.
  • Pluggable gateway layer; free events use native checkout, no redirect.
Guest · The reservation portal

The guest opens the portal

  • A live map: sold / locked / mine / remaining in one snapshot.
  • Pick a session; "Best Available" auto-selects the best block.
Guest · Selecting & locking a seat

Pick a seat — it locks instantly

  • lockForUpdate() in an ACID transaction makes rivals wait, then 409 — no double-booking.
  • SeatLocked broadcasts over Pusher, flipping the seat on every connected screen in < 200 ms.
Guest · Payment

Pay — verified server-side

  • Redirect to SkipCash with an HMAC-signed payload.
  • The server polls status itself — the browser is never trusted to confirm.
Guest · The ticket

The ticket arrives

  • Each ticket carries a unique UUID barcode_hash.
  • A queued HTML email + a downloadable PDF with the QR.
Door · QR check-in

Scan at the door

  • A browser QR scanner — no proprietary hardware.
  • Rejects invalid / already-scanned; writes a ScanLog.
Organizer · The dashboard

The organizer sees it all

  • Capacity, tickets sold, revenue at a glance.
  • 7-day sales velocity + distribution by price tier.
Production & Commercial · Security

Auth

  • JWT in localStorage → HTTP-only cookies. The token is invisible to JavaScript — XSS theft is gone.
  • CSRF handshake before every mutating request.
  • Google OAuth 2.0 via Socialite · scoped embed tokens for iframes.
Production & Commercial · B2B SDK

A white-label engine

  • Shadow users — a partner's customer is proxied through a managed identity, no account.
  • Embeddable iframes + a documented REST SDK to publish venues & confirm orders.

Conclusion

The question, answered

🎨Draw → book
An interactive canvas that becomes a strict relational schema.
🔒Zero double-booking
Pessimistic locks + real-time sync, validated under load.
💳Trustless payments
Server-side verified, UUID barcodes, QR check-in.
🧩Commercial-ready
B2B SDK, embeds, admin panel, automated CD — live at getseat.dev.

  • Full multi-tenancy + observability.
